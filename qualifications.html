Ages 11–18 · Pearson Edexcel International GCSEs

The qualifications at the heart of NEO

Real qualifications, taught live online by qualified, subject-specialist teachers, with a named practitioner alongside every learner. We build the offer around young people who have found exams and classrooms hard — so wherever a route can be gentler, we take it.

How our qualifications work

Our spine is the Pearson Edexcel International GCSE — a globally recognised qualification taken by learners around the world. The first intakes in September 2026 are a Year 9 and a Year 10 cohort, with learners entered when they are ready rather than all at once.

Taught live, by specialists

Every course is delivered live online by qualified, subject-specialist teachers — not pre-recorded, and never left to a learner to work through alone.

Linear, with gentler routes

These International GCSEs are assessed at the end of the course. Where a coursework or portfolio route exists — in English and in Art — we use it to take the weight off the exam hall.

Stepping stones too

Functional Skills, ASDAN and a Discovery Phase sit alongside, for learners building back towards formal qualifications.

Honest about the small print

International GCSEs are not regulated by Ofqual. Exam entry fees are charged separately from tuition. We'll always tell you what a route does and doesn't include.

Our International GCSEs

Six Pearson Edexcel International GCSEs, chosen to give a broad, balanced and achievable set of qualifications. Each is shown with its Pearson specification code.

Pearson Edexcel · 4EA1

English Language A

Structure
Linear International GCSE (9–1)
Assessment
100% examination, or 60% exam + 40% coursework; optional Spoken Language Endorsement
You study
Reading non-fiction and literary texts; transactional and imaginative writing; a set anthology

Why it suits NEO: the coursework route lifts much of the exam pressure, and it builds the everyday reading and writing that unlock everything else.

Pearson Edexcel · 4ET1

English Literature

Structure
Linear International GCSE (9–1)
Assessment
100% examination, or 60% exam + 40% coursework
You study
Poetry, modern prose (including contemporary novels such as Klara and the Sun), modern drama and literary heritage texts

Why it suits NEO: stories are often a way back in, and the coursework route suits learners who freeze in exams but think deeply on the page.

Pearson Edexcel · 4MA1

Mathematics A

Structure
Linear International GCSE (9–1), Foundation or Higher tier
Assessment
Two written papers; calculator permitted
You study
Number, algebra, geometry and statistics, at the tier that fits the learner

Why it suits NEO: tiered entry means a learner meets maths where they actually are, not where a year group says they should be.

Pearson Edexcel · 4HB1

Human Biology

Structure
Linear International GCSE (9–1), untiered
Assessment
Two written examinations
You study
The human body and how it works — cells and systems, health, disease and inheritance

Why it suits NEO: a science rooted in the learner's own body — tangible and relevant, and a strong base for health and care pathways.

Planned Pearson Edexcel · 4SD0

Science (Double Award)

Structure
Linear International GCSE (9–1), untiered — worth two GCSEs
Assessment
Three written examinations across Biology, Chemistry and Physics
You study
A full, balanced science across all three disciplines, equivalent to Combined Science

Planned for a future intake as our science team grows. Human Biology leads our science offer at launch.

Pearson Edexcel · 4FA1

Art & Design (Fine Art)

Structure
International GCSE (9–1) — portfolio-based, no written exam
Assessment
A Personal Portfolio, plus an Externally-set Assignment with a 10-hour period of sustained focus
You study
Drawing, painting, mixed media, print, sculpture and lens-based media

Why it suits NEO: a full qualification earned through making, with no written exam — ideal for learners who express themselves better through their hands than under exam conditions.

Beyond the qualifications

Two strands every learner takes part in, woven through the week alongside subjects — because wellbeing and how we relate to the world matter as much as grades.

RSHE — Relationships, Sex and Health Education

Age-appropriate, statutory Relationships, Sex and Health Education, delivered with care for learners who have had difficult experiences. It runs as part of our Friday Cornerstones Day, and elements are offered free to the wider parent and learner community.

It isn't a graded qualification — it's part of how NEO keeps young people safe, informed and well.

Relational Intelligence

NEO's own take on staying human in a world of fast-changing technology: understanding intelligence as something deeper than a score — how we see, respond, connect and grow, with people and now with machines. Learners explore emotional grounding online, asking better questions, and engaging ethically with AI. No coding required.

Other routes

Not every learner arrives ready for a full GCSE, and that's expected. These routes meet learners where they are and build forward.

Functional Skills

Practical, recognised qualifications in English, Maths and Digital Skills — useful in their own right and a confident stepping stone towards GCSE.

ASDAN

Flexible, accredited short courses and programmes that build skills and evidence achievement at a learner's own pace — see our selection below.

Discovery Phase

For learners not yet ready for formal qualifications — a Cornerstones-led re-engagement phase that rebuilds confidence and the habit of learning.

ASDAN courses for our cohort

Flexible, portfolio-based courses delivered through our ASDAN centre membership — most at Entry Level 3 to Level 1, accrediting 10–60 hours of activity. They build confidence and real-world skills alongside GCSEs, can be completed at a learner's own pace with guided at-home tasks, and bank credit towards ASDAN's wider Personal Development Programmes.

Short Course · Entry 3–Level 1

Animal Care

10–60 hours · portfolio-based

Caring for animals — from pets at home to farm animals and wildlife — across modules on animal welfare, safety, the law, animals in the media and careers working with animals.

Why it fits: gentle, motivating and doable from home with a learner's own pet — animals are a proven route to calm and connection.

Short Course · Entry 3–Level 1

Careers & Experiencing Work

10–60 hours · mapped to the Gatsby Benchmarks

Self-development, exploring careers, considering apprenticeships and higher education, and planning work experience — across seven structured modules.

Why it fits: builds work-readiness and a sense of "what next" at the learner's own pace, with no classroom pressure.

Short Course · Fully digital

Esports

10–60 hours · delivered online

The world behind competitive gaming — building teams and tournaments, health, wellbeing and safety, branding and media, and the careers in a multi-billion-pound industry.

Why it fits: meets many of our learners exactly where their interest already is, and turns screen time into recognised skills.

Short Course · Entry 3–Level 1

Gardening

10–60 hours · portfolio-based

Practical growing and wellbeing — planting, growing food, wildlife, cooking your own produce, and careers in horticulture — across eight modules.

Why it fits: calming and hands-on, naturally suited to guided at-home activity, and a Cornerstones-friendly route to Movement, Nutrition and Reflection.

Short Course · Entry 3–Level 1

Personal Finance

Up to 60 hours · portfolio-based

Real-world money skills — budgeting, income and spending, planning ahead and making financial choices — across five practical modules.

Why it fits: life skills every young person needs, and an accessible, confidence-building way into applied maths.

Modular framework · SEND-focused

Towards Independence

80+ modules · mapped to the SEND code of practice

A flexible framework recognising small steps in personal, social, work-related and independent-living skills, built up module by module into a record of real achievement.

Why it fits: meets learners with complex needs exactly where they are, accrediting progress that other qualifications miss.

Post-16 · Level 3 · up to 28 UCAS points

Extended Project Qualification (EPQ)

~120 hours incl. a taught element · England & NI

An independent research project — a 5,000-word dissertation, or an artefact with a report — built around a topic or work placement the learner chooses.

Why it fits: a serious, UCAS-bearing qualification that rewards deep interest and independence — ideal for post-16 learners heading towards university.

Courses awarded by ASDAN, a registered charity and awarding organisation, delivered under ASDAN centre membership. Availability depends on learner readiness and course suitability.
